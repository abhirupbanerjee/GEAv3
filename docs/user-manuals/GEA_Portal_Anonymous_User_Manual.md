## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 What is the GEA Portal?

The Government Enterprise Architecture (GEA) Portal is the official digital platform for engaging with Government of Grenada services. You can use this portal to:

- **Browse** available government services
- **Rate and review** government services you have received
- **Submit complaints** about service quality
- **File formal grievances** that require follow-up
- **Track the status** of your complaints
- **Get help** from AI-powered assistants

### 1.2 Who Can Use This Portal?

Anyone can use the public features of this portal, including:

- Grenadian citizens
- Residents
- Business owners
- Tourists and visitors
- Students
- Anyone who has interacted with Government of Grenada services

### 1.3 Do I Need an Account?

**No!** You can use all the features in this manual without creating an account:

- Submit feedback anonymously
- File grievances and receive a tracking number
- Check the status of your ticket anytime
- Browse government services

**Benefits of Creating an Account:**
- View all your feedback and tickets in one place
- Add comments to your tickets
- See statistics and analytics
- Receive in-portal updates

For account features, see the Citizen User Manual.

### 1.4 What You Will Need

- A device with internet access (computer, tablet, or smartphone)
- A modern web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge)
- Your contact information (optional but recommended for follow-up)
- Your ticket number (if checking on a previous submission)

## 4. Submitting Service Feedback

### 4.1 When to Submit Feedback

Submit feedback after you have:

- Visited a government office
- Used an online government service
- Received assistance from a government department
- Completed any interaction with a Ministry, Department, or Agency (MDA)
- Experienced service delivery from any government entity

### 4.2 How to Submit Feedback

**Step 1: Access the Feedback Form**
- Click **"Feedback"** in the main navigation menu
- Or navigate to the portal and select "Submit Feedback"

**Step 2: Select the Service**
- Use the search box to find the service you received
- Type the service name or department
- Select the correct **Ministry/Department/Agency** and **Service** from the dropdown

**Step 3: Select Your Category**

Choose who you are (this helps us understand different user experiences):
- **Citizen** - Grenadian citizen
- **Business** - Business owner or representative
- **Government Employee** - Government staff member (as a service user)
- **Visitor/Tourist** - Tourist or visitor to Grenada
- **Student** - Student
- **Other** - Other category

**Step 4: Rate Your Experience**

Answer five rating questions by selecting 1 to 5 stars for each:

1. **Ease of Access** - How easy was it to access the service?
   - 1 star = Very difficult
   - 5 stars = Very easy

2. **Clarity** - Was the information and process clear?
   - 1 star = Very confusing
   - 5 stars = Very clear

3. **Timeliness** - Was the service provided in a reasonable time?
   - 1 star = Very slow
   - 5 stars = Very timely

4. **Trust** - Do you trust the service process?
   - 1 star = No trust at all
   - 5 stars = Complete trust

5. **Overall Satisfaction** - How satisfied are you overall?
   - 1 star = Very dissatisfied
   - 5 stars = Very satisfied

**Step 5: Add Comments (Optional)**
- Describe your experience in the text box
- Include specific details about:
  - What went well
  - What could be improved
  - Specific incidents or interactions
  - Suggestions for improvement
- Maximum 1,000 characters

**Step 6: Provide Contact Information (Optional)**
- Enter your **Name** (optional)
- Enter your **Email** (optional, but recommended for follow-up)
- Enter your **Phone Number** (optional)

**Note:** Providing contact information is optional but helps the department follow up if needed.

**Step 7: File as Grievance (Optional)**
- Check **"I want to file this as a formal grievance"** if:
  - You need a formal response
  - The issue requires follow-up
  - You want to track resolution
  - You experienced significant problems

**Step 8: Submit**
- Review your information
- Click the **"Submit Feedback"** button
- Wait for the confirmation message

### 4.3 After Submission

**What Happens Next:**

1. You'll see a **confirmation message** with your Feedback ID
2. Your feedback is recorded in the system
3. The relevant department receives your feedback

**If You Filed a Grievance OR Your Ratings Were Low:**
- A **ticket** is automatically created
- You receive a **Ticket Number** (format: YYYYMM-XXXXXX)
- Example: 202601-123456
- **SAVE THIS NUMBER** - you'll need it to check your ticket status

**Low Rating Automatic Escalation:**
- If your average rating is **2.0 stars or lower**, the system automatically creates a grievance ticket
- This ensures serious complaints get proper attention

### 4.4 Submission Limits

To prevent misuse, the system limits submissions:
- **Maximum 5 feedback submissions per hour** from the same device
- If you exceed this limit, wait an hour and try again

## 5. Filing a Grievance

### 5.1 What is a Grievance?

A **grievance** is a formal complaint that requires follow-up and resolution. Unlike general feedback, grievances:
- Create a tracking ticket
- Require a formal response from the department
- Have defined resolution timelines
- Can be tracked using your ticket number

### 5.2 When to File a Grievance

File a grievance when:
- You experienced poor service quality
- Your issue was not resolved
- You need a formal response
- The matter requires investigation
- You want to track resolution progress

### 5.3 How to File a Grievance

**Method 1: Via Feedback Form**
1. Follow the feedback submission steps (Section 4.2)
2. At Step 7, check the box: **"I want to file this as a formal grievance"**
3. Submit the form
4. You'll receive a **Ticket Number** on the confirmation page

**Method 2: Automatic Escalation**
1. Submit feedback with low ratings (average ≤ 2.0 stars)
2. The system automatically creates a grievance
3. You receive a ticket number on the confirmation page

### 5.4 What Happens After Filing

1. **Ticket Created** - Your grievance receives a unique ticket number
2. **Department Notified** - The responsible department is alerted
3. **Assignment** - The grievance is assigned to a staff member
4. **Investigation** - Staff review and work on your complaint
5. **Resolution** - The issue is addressed
6. **Closure** - You receive a final response

**Typical Timeline:**
- Standard grievances: 5-10 business days
- Complex issues: May take longer
- Urgent matters: Prioritized for faster response

### 5.5 Your Ticket Number

**Format:** YYYYMM-XXXXXX
- **YYYY** - Year (e.g., 2026)
- **MM** - Month (e.g., 01 for January)
- **XXXXXX** - Unique number

**Example:** 202601-123456

**Important:** Save this number! You'll need it to check your ticket status.

## 6. Tracking Your Ticket

### 6.1 When to Check Your Ticket

Check your ticket status:
- After submitting a grievance
- To see if there are updates
- To view the resolution
- To share status with others

### 6.2 How to Check Ticket Status

**Step 1: Access the Helpdesk**
- Click **"Helpdesk"** in the main navigation menu
- Or navigate to `/helpdesk`

**Step 2: Enter Your Ticket Number**
- Type your ticket number in the search box
- Format: YYYYMM-XXXXXX (e.g., 202601-123456)
- Click **"View Ticket Status"** or press Enter

**Step 3: View Ticket Information**

The ticket details page shows:
- **Ticket Number** - Your unique reference
- **Status** - Current state of your ticket
- **Created Date** - When you submitted the grievance
- **Subject** - Brief description of your issue
- **Description** - Full details of your complaint
- **Priority** - Urgency level assigned by staff
- **Assigned Entity** - Department handling your case

### 6.3 Understanding Ticket Status

| Status | Meaning | What It Means for You |
|--------|---------|----------------------|
| **Open** | Ticket received, awaiting review | Your grievance has been logged. Wait for staff to review. |
| **Assigned** | Assigned to a staff member | Someone is now responsible for handling your case. |
| **In Progress** | Staff are actively working on it | Your issue is being investigated and worked on. |
| **Pending Customer** | Waiting for your response | Staff need more information from you. Check for messages. |
| **Resolved** | Issue has been addressed | The department has addressed your concern. Review the resolution. |
| **Closed** | Ticket is complete | Your grievance has been finalized. No further action. |

### 6.4 Ticket Timeline

The ticket detail page shows a timeline of activity:
- **Created** - When you submitted
- **Status Changes** - Each time the status was updated
- **Resolution Comment** - Final response from staff (when resolved)

### 6.5 What You CAN See (Public View)

Without logging in, you can see:
- Basic ticket information
- Current status
- Status change history
- Resolution comments (when ticket is resolved or closed)

### 6.6 What You CANNOT See (Public View)

The public view does not show:
- Internal staff notes
- Detailed activity logs
- Ability to add comments
- Private communications between staff

**Want More Access?**
Create a free Citizen Portal account to:
- View all your tickets in one place
- Add comments to open tickets
- Receive in-portal notifications
- See complete activity history

### 6.7 If You Lost Your Ticket Number

If you provided your email when submitting feedback:
- Check your email inbox for the confirmation
- The ticket number should be in the confirmation email

If you didn't provide contact information:
- Unfortunately, there's no way to retrieve the ticket number
- Create a Citizen Portal account for future submissions to track everything

## 7. Using QR Codes

### 7.1 What Are QR Codes For?

QR codes are displayed at government service locations to make feedback submission faster and easier. When you scan a QR code:
- The service information is automatically filled in
- You can immediately rate your experience
- Feedback is submitted while the experience is fresh

### 7.2 Where to Find QR Codes

Look for QR codes at government facilities:
- **Reception desks** - At the entrance
- **Waiting areas** - On posters or signs
- **Service counters** - Near payment or service delivery points
- **Exit points** - As you leave
- **Information boards** - On government facility signage

### 7.3 How to Use QR Codes

**Step 1: Open Your Phone Camera**
- Most modern smartphones can scan QR codes with the camera app
- No special app needed (on most phones)

**Step 2: Point at the QR Code**
- Open your camera app
- Point it at the QR code
- Hold your phone steady
- Keep the QR code in the center of your screen

**Step 3: Tap the Link**
- A notification or link will appear on your screen
- Tap the link to open the feedback form
- The service and location are automatically filled in

**Step 4: Complete Your Feedback**
- The service is already selected for you
- Rate your experience (1-5 stars for each question)
- Add comments about your experience
- File as grievance if needed
- Submit your feedback

### 7.4 Benefits of QR Code Feedback

- **Faster** - Service information pre-filled
- **Convenient** - Submit right after your visit
- **Accurate** - Feedback while the experience is fresh
- **Location-specific** - Helps track quality at specific offices

### 7.5 QR Code Troubleshooting

**QR Code Won't Scan:**
- Ensure good lighting
- Hold your phone steady
- Make sure the QR code is not damaged or faded
- Try moving closer or further away

**No Camera App:**
- Download a free QR code scanner app from your app store
- Or manually type the portal URL into your browser

The GEA Portal may include AI-powered assistants (chatbots) to help you:
- Find information about services
- Navigate the portal
- Answer common questions
- Guide you through processes

**Note:** AI assistant availability depends on your portal configuration.

**Method 1: Chat Widget**
- Look for a chat icon (usually in the bottom-right corner)
- Click the icon to open the chat window

**Method 2: Dedicated Page**

### 8.3 What AI Assistants Can Help With

Common uses:
- **Finding Services** - "Where do I get a business license?"
- **Process Questions** - "How do I submit feedback?"

### 8.4 How to Use AI Assistants

**Step 1: Start a Conversation**
- Type your question in natural language

**Step 2: Follow the Guidance**
- The assistant will respond with information or questions

**Step 3: Get to Your Goal**
- The assistant may guide you to:
  - Specific pages on the portal

  - Step-by-step instructions

### 8.5 Tips for Using AI Assistants

**Do:**
- Ask clear, specific questions
- Provide context about your situation
- Follow suggested links
- Ask follow-up questions if unclear

**Don't:**
- Share sensitive personal information

## 10. Frequently Asked Questions

### General Questions

**Q: Do I need to create an account to use the portal?**
A: No! You can submit feedback, file grievances, and check ticket status without an account. Accounts are optional but provide additional features.

**Q: Is my feedback anonymous?**
A: Yes, if you submit without logging in. You can optionally provide contact information for follow-up.

**Q: How much does it cost to use the portal?**
A: The portal is completely free to use for everyone.

**Q: Can I use the portal on my mobile phone?**
A: Yes! The portal is fully mobile-friendly and works on all modern smartphones and tablets.

### Feedback Questions

**Q: What if I don't know the exact service name?**
A: Use the Services directory to browse and find the service, or search using keywords. You can also describe the service in your feedback comments.

**Q: Can I submit feedback for multiple services?**
A: Yes, but submit separate feedback for each service to help departments track specific service quality.

**Q: What if I want to provide feedback but don't want to file a grievance?**
A: Simply don't check the "file as grievance" box. Your feedback will still be recorded and reviewed.

**Q: How long does it take to get a response?**
A: Response times vary:
- General feedback: Reviewed but may not receive individual response
- Grievances: Typically 5-10 business days
- Urgent matters: Prioritized for faster response

**Q: Can I submit multiple pieces of feedback?**
A: Yes, but the system limits submissions to 5 per hour to prevent misuse.

### Grievance & Ticket Questions

**Q: What's the difference between feedback and a grievance?**
A: Feedback is a rating and review. A grievance is a formal complaint that creates a tracking ticket and requires a response.

**Q: I lost my ticket number. How do I find it?**
A: Check your email (if you provided one). Otherwise, create a Citizen Portal account for future submissions to keep everything tracked.

**Q: Can I cancel or modify a ticket after submission?**
A: You cannot modify submissions directly. To update, create a Citizen Portal account and add a comment to your ticket.

**Q: What if my ticket status hasn't changed in a long time?**
A: If there's no update after 10 business days, contact the assigned department or DTA support (see Contact Information).

**Q: Can someone else check my ticket status?**
A: Yes, anyone with your ticket number can check the status using the public Helpdesk.

### Technical Questions

**Q: What browsers are supported?**
A: Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge. We recommend using the latest version of your browser.

**Q: Why can't I submit feedback?**
A: You may have reached the hourly limit (5 submissions per hour). Wait and try again later.

**Q: The QR code isn't working. What should I do?**
A: Ensure good lighting, hold your phone steady, and make sure the code isn't damaged. You can also manually navigate to the feedback page.

**Q: I'm having technical issues with the portal. Who do I contact?**
A: Contact DTA technical support at alerts.dtahelpdesk@gmail.com with details about the issue.
